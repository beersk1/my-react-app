    The Facts

    Shocking Helmet Hygiene Facts

    What you don't know about your helmet could be affecting your health

    Bacteria Hotspot

    A helmet can hold 20,000+ bacteria per square inch—more than a toilet seat.

    Sweat & Oil Trap

    Sweat, oil, and dust get trapped in the padding, creating an ideal environment for bacteria.

    Ineffective Cleaning

    Traditional washing methods don't eliminate bacteria from the inner lining.

    Health Risks

    Dirty helmets can cause skin irritations, acne, and other health issues.

    The Problem

    What's Wrong with Current Cleaning Methods?

    Traditional helmet cleaning practices do more harm than good

    Water Damage

    Water cleaning weakens the helmet's foam, affecting its safety in case of impact.

    Harsh Chemicals

    Some cleaning chemicals cause skin irritation and damage helmet materials.

    Poor Cleaning Practices

    Helmets are rarely cleaned properly, leading to bad odors, acne, and hygiene issues.
